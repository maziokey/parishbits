Our History

Rev. Fr. Moses Jimbili - Parish Priest

Preparatory to establishing a worshipping community in Jikwoyi, Rev. Fr. Richard Devine, SMA said the first Holy Mass in Jikwoyi on the 22nd of September 1992. That began a tradition of monthly masses in Jikwoyi. Following that was the erection of a temporary church building initiated by Fr. Devine in November 1993. And then in 1994, Fr. Devine named the church St. Kevin’s after his home parish in Ireland.

When Fr. Devine returned to Ireland, Rev. Fr. Jerry Amman, SJ oversaw St. Kevin’s Church, along with 13 other churches under St. Patrick’s Parish, Karshi at the time. After Fr. Jerry came Rev. Fr. Anthony Onyeso. After Fr. Onyeso, the Archdiocese handed St. Patrick’s parish over to the Missionary Society of Saint Paul (MSP). Rev. Fr. Okpon, MSP became the Parish Priest, succeeded by Rev. Fr. Anthony Afiangide, MSP, then Rev. Fr. Dominic Oguama, MSP who created St. Kevin’s Parish out of St. Patrick’s in July 2011.

Messrs. Joseph Odey and Soribe Ambrose, Station Council leaders, were instrumental in St. Kevin’s elevation to a parish by His Grace, Most Rev. Dr. John Olorunfemi Onaiyekan, Archbishop of the Metropolitan See of Abuja.

The first Parish Priest of St. Kevin’s Parish was Rev. Fr. Ali Edogbanya; he led a pastoral team comprising Rev. Frs. Daniel Ihunanya, MSP, Christopher Inegbenoghu, and Stephen Akpan that administered the parish for five years, then handing over to Rev. Fr. Daniel Msugh Agber in 2006. Fr. Agber’s team had Rev. Frs. Alexander Ogbenufe, Christopher Nnubia, and Mathew Kwaggas. Rev. Fr. Aloysius Isua succeeded Fr. Agber as parish priest, himself handing over to Rev. Fr. Moses Jimbili – the current parish priest – in 2018. Mr. Augustine Ager is the parish Catechist; he succeeded Mr. John Obodoagu – the pioneer catechist of the parish.
